# Rationale — why a fractal, and how it stays subtle

The book's methodology is itself fractal. It rests on the **Job Graph** — a nested
graph of a customer's goals — and the value-creation mechanics repeat *self-similarly*
at every level, from the smallest interaction up to the customer's whole life. The same
pattern recurs at every scale.

So the cover's motif is not decoration: it's **the book's central idea made visible** —
*quietly*. The brief and skill are emphatic that the fractal must be **barely
noticeable**: a background texture, discoverable on a second glance, never the hero.
Typography leads; the motif is felt more than seen.

## The three directions

1. **Typographic-only / almost-only** — type carries everything; the self-similar idea
   is at most a whisper. Safest route to "premium business classic."
2. **Graph-fractal haze** — the Job Graph as *atmosphere*: a nested, self-similar field
   that reads as texture, **not** a literal diagram (the brief explicitly warns against
   literalness).
3. **Geometric self-similarity** — restrained recursion (Sierpiński / Pythagoras tree /
   subdivision), low-contrast, embedded.

Selection follows the skill's rubric; a direction must score ≥4 on *premium feel* and
*thumbnail readability* to be a finalist.
