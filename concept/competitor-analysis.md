# Competitor analysis

> **Checkpoint 0** — filled in with the user. Input: **1 strong** + **3 weak**
> competitor covers. Goal: extract concrete *do / don't* that steer the motif, palette,
> and typographic decisions **before** the contact sheet.

## The strong one — why it works

A competitor cover for **the same book** (top-liked in Zamesin's audience channel):
deep warm near-black ground, cream/gold high-contrast serif title stacked on three
lines (left-aligned, dominant), serif subtitle a step quieter, author in small tracked
caps at the bottom. A faint dendritic/branching fractal spreads across the upper field
at **very low contrast**, with soft light pooling — reads as depth, not poster.

**Works because:** premium & cinematic palette; fractal kept as subliminal texture;
textbook hierarchy (title owns it, subtitle supports, author whispers); pleasant to
linger on. Validates our **Palette B (deep monochrome)** + barely-there fractal.

**Fails because (user's read, matches the brief):** the fractal grows out of a literal
human **head/crown** → slides into *neuro / psych / brain* territory, which the brief
explicitly forbids, and the "idea grows from the mind" metaphor isn't really about the
*product* approach.

**Take the atmosphere, change the anchor:** keep darkness, soft light, ultra-low-
contrast fractal, depth — but the motif must read as **structure / self-similarity
(nested goals / Job Graph)**, embedded as an all-over field, **not** emanating from a
person. Typographically: go **lighter weight + higher contrast** than a default heavy
serif; refined, airy, confident.

## The three weak ones — why they fail

All three read **cheaper / AI-slop** to the audience-owner. Shared failure: the concept
is shown **too literally** — as a discrete object or diagram — so it competes with the
title and stops being atmosphere.

1. **K1 — navy node-graph on cream.** Elegant, calm, on-point, but the self-similar
   structure is drawn as an explicit, evenly-spaced **graph of circles** → it reads as
   **infographic**, not atmosphere, and competes with the title. (Confirms the brief's
   "no literal Job Graph schematic.")
2. **K2 — red nested rectangles.** Conceptually nice ("levels of a product"), but the
   outline **frames are decorative** — flat, no real depth, **no clear hook**.
3. **K3 — dark ground + detailed romanesco illustration.** Beautiful drawing, but the
   illustration **eats the book** → an "art poster about nature," not premium business
   nonfiction. The motif has become the hero.

## Extracted rules

**Do**
- Deep, warm, near-black palette reads premium & cinematic (audience-validated).
- Keep the motif **atmospheric, embedded, ultra-low-contrast**; use soft light for depth.
- Let a refined **high-contrast serif** lead; title dominant, subtitle a quiet step down,
  author in small tracked caps.
- Self-similarity should be **felt**, discovered on a second look.

**Don't**
- No explicit/even **node graph** (infographic feel, competes with title).
- No decorative **frames/borders** standing in for "structure" (flat, no hook).
- No centered, detailed **illustration** of a fractal (becomes an art poster).
- No literal **head/brain/neuro** anchor (off-concept; brief forbids brains).
- Nothing that announces the concept too fast — if you read the idea instantly, it's too loud.

## Implications for our directions

- **Typographic-only / almost-only** — *safest, now front-runner.* Deep-monochrome type
  composition with at most a whisper of texture. Hard to make slop; easy to make premium.
- **Graph-fractal haze** — *high-risk; survives only as true haze.* K1 is its failure
  mode. Must never resolve into visible nodes/edges — only an out-of-focus, low-frequency
  field behind the type, far from the title.
- **Geometric self-similarity** — *reframe away from K2.* No outline frames. If used, it
  must create **depth/light** (e.g. subtle tonal subdivision, soft recursive shading),
  not decorative borders.
- **(Natural fractal)** — demoted to *texture-only*; never a centered object (K3).

**Net recipe to pursue first:** *the strong cover minus the head* — deep monochrome +
refined high-contrast serif leading + an ultra-subtle, embedded, atmospheric self-similar
texture, soft light for depth.
