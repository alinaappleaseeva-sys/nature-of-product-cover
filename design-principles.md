# Design Principles — invariants (do not break)

These are the **fixed** constraints for the *Nature of Product* cover, distilled from
the brief and the project skill. They do not change between iterations. The *current
chosen solution* (specific colors, fonts, sizes) lives in [`design.md`](design.md);
this file is the guardrail and the brief for any agent working on the cover.

## Exact text & hierarchy (preserve wording)

- **Title (dominant):** Nature of Product
- **Subtitle (small, elegant, secondary):** How to Build Products So Valuable They Look Like Magic
- **Author (smallest, bottom):** Ivan Zamesin

The title must own the cover and read at thumbnail size. Subtitle clearly secondary.
Author supports the hierarchy, never dominates.

## Non-negotiables

- Typography **leads**; the motif supports it, never competes.
- The fractal / self-similar layer stays **subtle** — discoverable on a second glance,
  never the hero, never high-contrast, never centered as an object.
- Legible and convincing at **thumbnail** size.
- **≤ 2 font families.** Title in **title case**, not all caps. Serif-led title.
- **Tight palette:** background + text + **one** restrained accent.
- Must not look like generic AI / startup / self-published design.
- Generous negative space; editorial, not promotional.
- If concept cleverness fights thumbnail readability → **readability wins.**

## Avoid completely

Brains, rockets, up-arrows, gears, light bulbs, glowing nodes, wireframe networks as a
dominant motif, neon gradients, cyberpunk, glossy 3D blobs, text shadows, bevels,
metallic effects, fake-luxury tropes, loud decorative symmetry, clutter that explains
the concept too literally. No paid fonts. No default fonts (Papyrus, Comic Sans,
Arial/Times, Trajan). No literal Job-Graph schematic.

## Review rubric (from the skill)

Score each direction 1–5 on: **premium feel · typographic hierarchy · thumbnail
readability · concept subtlety · shelf presence · originality without gimmicks.**
5 = competition-ready, 4 = strong/needs polish, 3 = promising/unresolved, 2 = weak,
1 = reject. **A direction below 4 on *premium feel* or *thumbnail readability* is not a
finalist.**

## Final check (before calling a direction strong)

Works without explanation · premium in grayscale · works at thumbnail · fractal stays
subordinate · typography carries the cover · not self-published · not AI-looking.

## Output / process discipline

Narrow exploration: 3 strong directions, **≤2 palette variants per direction**, **≤2
type pairings per round**. No giant matrices. Smallest effective change first. Rank
directions, reject weak ones plainly.

## Production constraints

- Print: 6 × 9 in, 300 DPI, CMYK, 0.125 in bleed (doc 6.25 × 9.25 in = 1875 × 2775 px).
- Ebook: 1600 × 2560 px, RGB.
- Editable source required; master keeps **live text** until final export, outlines only
  as a separate final print artifact.
- Fonts: SIL OFL / Google Fonts only (free for commercial use).
